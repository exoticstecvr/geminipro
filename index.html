The Industrial Revolution: A Comprehensive Study

I. The Socio-Economic Genesis

The Industrial Revolution began in Great Britain due to a convergence of geographic and economic factors. The availability of coal and a surplus of labor shifted production from homes to massive urban centers.

II. Steam and Mechanical Advance

James Watt’s refinement of the steam engine enabled factories to move away from rivers. This centralization led to the rise of major industrial hubs in coal-rich regions.

III. The Global Legacy

By the late 19th century, steel and electricity introduced the Second Industrial Revolution, laying the groundwork for our modern digital and globalized economy.
